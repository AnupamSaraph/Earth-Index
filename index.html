Finding Home

Less than one in 60 billion planets in the universe can be home to life. Seen from the vastness of space, searching for home is like searching for a needle in a haystack.

Our Earth

Our Earth is home to between 8 million to a trillion species - microbes, plants, and animals. It offers a diversity of habitats - arctic, tundra, aquatic, coastal, ocean, desert, forest, grassland, mountain, steppes, volcanos, wetlands, extreme habitats, micro-habitats - or all the environmental conditions for different life-forms to keep the Earth habitable and make home.

Interactions of people with each-other

Interactions of people with each-other

Interactions that enable the people in a country to interact with each other without any threats, violence, or need to seek out other lands for their need to interact with each other, contribute to the liveability of our planet.

The more the proportion of people in a country that do not have to meet their needs for interaction in other lands, the more the country contributes to the liveability of our planet.

Interactions of people with other species

Interactions of people with other species

Interactions of people with other species that leave space for the other species - and show restraint in occupying all the land, air, and water in our country - contribute to the liveability of our planet.

The more the space that a country leaves for other species, the more it contributes to the liveability of our planet.

Interactions of people with ecosystems

Interactions of people with ecosystems

Interactions of people with ecosystems that show restraint and reduce the aggression caused by exploitation, encroachment, and pollution on every unit of land,while conducting their activities contribute to the liveability of our planet.

The less the exploitation, encroachment, or pollution per unit of land in a country, the more it contributes to the liveability of our planet.

Interactions of people with human designed systems

Interactions of people with human designed systems

Interactions of people with the systems and organizations that they have created to provide them to live peaceful and healthy lives, ensure inclusion, and performance - their governments, businesses, and non-governmental organizations - contribute to the liveability of our planet.

The better the interactions of the people with the systems and organizations that they have created, the longer they live.

What Makes Earth Livable?

The interactions we have with each other, with other species, with other ecosystems, and with organizations we create contribute to making our Earth livable.

Earth Index

The Earth Index is an average of the scores for interactions people have with each other, with other life forms, with ecosystems, and with their human designed systems and organizations. It is an indicator of the investment the country makes to the liveability of our home, Earth. We report here the 20 countries that had the highest and and 20 that had the lowest Earth Index scores in 1992 and 2019.

Change in Earth Index

The actions, policies and culture of a country alter the interactions its people have with each other, with other life forms, with ecosystems, and with their human designed systems and organizations. Decisions and actions change from year-to-year influencing the short-term contributions. Policies change more slowly and, therefore, influence the contributions over the medium-term. Cultures last long and, therefore provide resilience to the contributions over the long-term. We report here the the change in Earth Index of countries between 1992 and 2019 (long-term), 2009 and 2019 (medium-term), and 2018 and 2019 (short-term).
